# Episode Agent Pack: Saint Seraphim of Sarov

## Research Agent Input
Channel lane: The Saints
Topic: Saint Seraphim of Sarov
Audience: Orthodox Christians, Catholics interested in saints, Christian history viewers, spiritually curious viewers
Freshness: stable historical/religious sources; verify modern web source URLs before citation
Core sources:
- OCA Saint Seraphim of Sarov
- Britannica Saint Seraphim of Sarov
- Public-domain Orthodox lives where available
- Orthodox monastery/jurisdictional sources for cross-checking

Research instruction:
Create a fact-aware research brief. Separate verified historical facts from Orthodox hagiographic tradition. Note birth-year discrepancy between Britannica and OCA without making it a major narration issue.

## Story Architect Instruction
Build a complete no-upper-cap long-form story around the paradox of Saint Seraphim: severe ascetic struggle producing luminous joy. Use the copper cross, forest cell, and greeting of joy as recurring motifs.

## Scriptwriting Instruction
Write a full narration script for a reverent cinematic documentary. Do not rush the story. Do not copy OCA or Britannica prose. Frame miracle accounts as Orthodox tradition. Avoid generic motivational language; make scenes concrete.

## Visual Prompt Instruction
Create historically respectful Russian Orthodox monastic visuals: Kursk church construction, Sarov monastery, winter forest, humble cell, prayer rope, candlelight, pilgrims, icon lamp. Avoid fantasy monk styling.

## Metadata Instruction
Package the video around curiosity and reverence, not shock. Strong title direction: "The Russian Monk Who Greeted Everyone With Joy" or "The Forest Monk Who Found Joy in Silence."

## QC Instruction
Check for:
- copied source phrasing
- unsupported miracle certainty
- disrespectful religious framing
- incorrect dates presented too confidently
- generic visuals not matching Russian Orthodoxy
- script under 8 minutes

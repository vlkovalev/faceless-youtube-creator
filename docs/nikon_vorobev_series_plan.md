## Editorial Position

This should be a Saints mini-series on Hegumen Nikon Vorobiev, not a direct remake of the linked YouTube narration. Use the YouTube video as a discovery lead only. Build episodes from written sources, especially Pravenc for chronology and Predanie for the letter tradition.

Tone: quiet, severe, compassionate, spiritually practical. Nikon's draw is not spectacle; it is repentance, discernment, restraint, and pity for others under 20th-century pressure.

## Proposed Mini-Series

1. **The Elder Who Left Philosophy for Repentance**
   - Script ID: 20
   - Hook: a brilliant young seeker leaves abstract systems for a life of prayer, survives repression, and becomes a teacher of repentance through letters.
   - Status: script v1 created.

2. **Do Not Reveal Yourself to Anyone**
   - Focus: humility, self-exposure, praise, spiritual vanity, and Nikon's practical warnings about self-display.
   - Source base: the linked YouTube theme plus published letter collections.

3. **The Prison Camp and the Hidden Priest**
   - Focus: arrest in 1933, sentence to five years, release in 1937, and the inner steadiness that survived political terror.
   - Source base: Pravenc chronology; avoid sensational prison claims unless sourced.

4. **We Have Been Left Repentance**
   - Focus: Nikon's central teaching on repentance, sobriety, passions, humility, and the tradition of Ignatius Brianchaninov.
   - Source base: Predanie pages for "Nam ostavleno pokayanie" and "Kak zhit segodnya."

5. **Letters to Spiritual Children**
   - Focus: how a small parish priest became widely known after death through letters to monks, students, clergy, and laypeople.
   - Source base: Predanie works hub and selected letters.

6. **Pity One Another**
   - Focus: Nikon's final counsel, illness, death on September 7, 1963, and his pastoral insistence on mercy over judgment.
   - Source base: Predanie biographical material and Pravenc chronology.

## Visual Language

- Real-source priority: Nikon portrait/photo, Gzhatsk/Gagarin area, Russian churches, handwritten letters, Soviet-era documents, monastery/interior imagery, icons of Christ, Ignatius Brianchaninov references where licensed.
- Generated fallback: restrained Soviet-era parish interiors, a desk with letters, candlelit study, snowy roads, prison-camp silhouettes without graphic violence.
- Avoid: exaggerated gulag spectacle, invented miracle visuals, modern glossy influencer-style thumbnails.

## Production Notes

- Keep all quotes short and source-attributed. Prefer paraphrase.
- Use "Hegumen Nikon Vorobiev" in English metadata, with alternate spellings "Vorobyov" and "Vorobiov" in tags.
- Make clear that he is a Russian Orthodox spiritual writer and pastor; do not call him formally canonized unless a reliable source supports that status.
- Do not use YouTube end screens as the closing dependency. End each episode with an in-video verbal support CTA, author appreciation, and a short blessing.

# Saints Prayer Companion Format

Purpose: after each saint-life documentary, produce a companion prayer video people can pray/read with on-screen text and a reverent icon or icon-style still.

## Episode Pattern

1. Opening title: saint name, prayer type, feast/commemoration if known.
2. Short pastoral context: what life circumstances people commonly bring to this saint in prayer.
3. Prayer reading: Akathist preferred. If no saint-specific Akathist is available, use an appropriate Orthodox prayer, canon, troparion/kontakion, or a Theotokos Akathist tied to a relevant icon.
4. On-screen text: large readable text, one prayer unit at a time, with the icon visible throughout.
5. Closing: brief invitation to pray with attention, no medical or guaranteed-outcome claims.

## Voice Policy

- Prefer a real priest, deacon, monk, chanter, or church reader only when the recording is explicitly licensed, public-domain, or permissioned for YouTube reuse.
- Do not clone, imitate, or style-copy a real Orthodox priest or monk without explicit consent.
- If no permissioned recording exists, use an original, non-impersonating narration voice or commission a reader.
- Keep the voice reverent, slow, low-distraction, and prayerful.

## Source Policy

- Verify prayer text reuse rights before placing full text on screen.
- Keep source URLs and license notes in the companion queue entry.
- If the prayer text is copyrighted or terms are unclear, either request permission, use a public-domain/permissioned source, or prepare a recording-only draft for manual approval.

## Pastoral Language

Use careful phrasing:
- "People often pray this in times of..."
- "Tradition associates this saint with..."
- "This is not a promise of a specific outcome."

Avoid:
- guaranteed healing claims
- superstition-style promises
- replacing pastoral, medical, or professional help

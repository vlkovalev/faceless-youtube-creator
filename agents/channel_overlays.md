# Channel-Specific Agent Overlays

## Global Video Closing Rule

Use this rule for every channel video unless the user explicitly overrides it for a specific upload.

- Do not rely on YouTube end screens as the required closing experience.
- Each video must end with an in-video closing screen, verbal close, or both.
- The verbal close must ask viewers to support the channel by subscribing, include author appreciation, and finish with a short blessing-style signoff.
- Default line: "This channel needs your support. Please subscribe, leave a word of appreciation for the author, and may God bless you."
- If the channel also needs a practical action, such as downloading code or commenting a use case, put that before the default support line.

## Corporate Shadows Overlay

Use this overlay for business scandal, corporate history, monopoly, fraud, and dark business documentary episodes.

### Tone
Dark, cinematic, investigative, morally clear, but not reckless.

### Required Framing
- Serious allegations must be sourced.
- Use phrases like "critics argued," "investigators found," "according to," or "historically, this led to" when needed.
- Avoid defamatory certainty about living people without strong sources.
- Avoid graphic or exploitative framing.
- End with the global video closing rule. Do not depend on end screens or "click here" language.

### Story Ingredients
- Hidden incentive
- Victim or public cost
- Boardroom decision
- Mechanism of power
- Consequence
- Modern echo

### Visual Style
- Moody boardrooms
- Archival documents
- Maps, factories, courtrooms, ledgers
- High contrast, restrained color
- No generic stock smiles

### Thumbnail Formula

Left side: dark skyscraper + red warning symbol. Right side: billion-dollar chart collapsing. Center text: "$9 BILLION LIE" / "THE COLLAPSE" / "CORPORATE SHADOWS". AI prompt: "Corporate skyscraper in darkness, financial collapse, dramatic red lighting, cinematic documentary poster, ultra realistic, Netflix style, high contrast, mysterious atmosphere."

### Monetization-Safe Sourcing
- Use footage from Pexels Videos, Pixabay Videos, Mixkit
- Combine with AI-generated scenes, animated charts, document mockups, and sound design
- No copyrighted movie clips

## The Saints Overlay

Use this overlay for Orthodox/Catholic saints, monks, elders, martyrs, desert fathers, Athos, Russian monasticism, and Christian history.

### Tone
Reverent, cinematic, careful, spiritually serious, emotionally human.

### Runtime Rule
Minimum 8 minutes for monetized long-form. No upper limit. The full story or tale determines length.

### Required Framing
- Distinguish history, tradition, testimony, and legend.
- Do not mock miracles, relics, icons, asceticism, monastic obedience, or liturgical practices.
- Do not turn denominational differences into cheap conflict.
- Avoid claiming modern certainty where sources only provide hagiographic tradition.
- Modern copyrighted biographies may be summarized, not copied.
- End with the global video closing rule.

### Story Ingredients
- World before the saint
- Calling or conversion
- Spiritual struggle
- Obedience or ascetic formation
- Decisive witness
- Death/repose or martyrdom
- Church memory and legacy
- Meaning for viewers today
- Verbal support CTA: "This channel needs your support. Please subscribe, leave a word of appreciation for the author, and may God bless you."

### Visual Style
- Candlelit cells
- Icons and oil lamps
- Monasteries, forests, caves, sea cliffs
- Prayer ropes, manuscripts, bells
- Reverent composition
- No fantasy costumes or generic medieval nonsense

## SaaS Autopilot Overlay

Use this overlay for future software, AI workflow, SaaS, automation, and business tutorial channels.

### Tone
Practical, proof-based, clean, business-useful.

### Required Framing
- Show actual workflow or realistic example.
- Disclose affiliate bias in metadata when relevant.
- Avoid exaggerated income claims.
- Update old tool tutorials when interfaces change.
- End with the global video closing rule. Keep any download-code instruction before the support line.

### Story Ingredients
- Problem
- Workflow
- Tool stack
- Step-by-step setup
- Test result
- Cost and limitation
- Template or next action

## Saints Miracle Account Overlay

- Miracle accounts are welcome when they are part of the saint's received life or tradition.
- Frame them as Church memory, hagiographic tradition, testimony, or later devotion as appropriate.
- The goal is spiritual meaning, not paranormal spectacle.
- Never promise viewers healing or guaranteed intervention.
- Never use miracle claims to mock nonbelievers or attack another Christian tradition.
- When the source is late, disputed, or embellished, soften the wording.
- Miracles can be strong hooks, but the episode must still tell the full life.
